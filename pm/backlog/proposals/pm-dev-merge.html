! Problem & Context

Product engineers currently use two separate plugins (PM and Dev) that don't share state. The handoff from groomed idea to implementation is implicit — each plugin guesses at the other's state. Research validated through pm:groom produces rich ACs, competitive context, and EM feasibility notes, but dev:dev-epic can't detect or leverage any of it. The result: redundant ceremony (re-brainstorming, re-reviewing) and lost context.

Market signal: The "product engineer" role is real and growing. Anthropic's Head of Product (Cat Wu): "Designers ship code, engineers make product decisions, PMs build prototypes." Gibson Consultants (March 2026) predicts a "messy middle" where PMs increasingly build with AI tools. No competitor offers integrated research→grooming→implementation→merge — this is unoccupied whitespace.

Strategy override: This conflicts with Non-Goal #1 ("PM ends at the groomed ticket"), overridden because agentic coding is blurring PM/engineer roles. The new positioning: "Structured workflows for the product engineer, on top of whatever AI coding assistant they already use."

Three goals: (1) Build valuable products, (2) Build efficiently, (3) Manage cognitive load.

? Open Questions

Questions the decision-maker should be prepared to discuss:

  • What is the upgrade path for existing PM-only users? Does the merged plugin ship under the same name, or is it a new install?
  • Why do the full merger now vs. shipping the handoff integration first (which the research itself recommends as the primary value)?
  • How do you measure success without a baseline? What is the current step count for groomed vs ungroomed issues?
  • What happens if context size degrades skill quality? Is there a rollback plan?
  • What are we NOT doing because we are doing this? (PostHog integration, Google Docs, analytics ingestion are all on the priority list.)
