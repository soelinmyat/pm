## Content Strategy Signals

### Blog Cadence and Topics

Productboard maintains an active blog covering several categories:
- **Company & Product:** Spark announcements, product launches, company news
- **Product Management:** Educational content on PM practices, workflows, AI in PM
- **Engineering:** Technical blog posts
- **Product Operations:** Operational PM guides
- **Product Excellence:** Best practices
- **Product Leaders:** Executive-level content

Recent content is heavily weighted toward AI and Spark:
- "Spark Ignites: The Future of Product Management Is Here"
- "How to Do Product Discovery with AI"
- "Technical AI Terms Product Managers Need to Know"
- "How AI is Reshaping Product Operations"
- "Building AI Products: 4 Core Lessons from Productboard's AI PMs"
- "Context (Not AI) Is the Real Competitive Advantage"

### SEO Plays

- **Thought leadership content:** Publishing educational PM content to capture informational intent searches. Heavy emphasis on AI-related topics to establish authority in emerging "AI for PM" category.
- **Integration landing pages:** Dedicated pages for each integration (Jira, Slack, Salesforce, etc.) to capture integration-specific searches.
- **Comparison defense:** While Productboard does not appear to publish its own "Productboard vs X" pages, competitors are aggressively publishing these. This is a potential SEO gap.
- **Support knowledge base:** Extensive help center at support.productboard.com provides long-tail content and user-intent coverage.
- **Press and PR amplification:** Major product launches (Spark) are accompanied by press releases on GlobeNewsWire distributed to Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance, and tech media, generating high-authority backlinks.

### Content Gaps

- **No visible "vs" comparison pages:** Competitors (Monday.com, Canny, Featurebase) are publishing "Productboard vs X" content. Productboard does not appear to publish defensive comparison content.
- **Limited Spark-specific SEO content:** Despite the homepage pivot, there is limited blog content specifically optimized for "AI product management agent" or "AI PM tool" keywords beyond launch announcements.
- **No community/user-generated content program:** No evidence of a customer stories hub, case studies optimized for SEO, or community content program beyond the blog.
