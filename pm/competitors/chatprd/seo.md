## Content Strategy Signals

### Blog Cadence and Format
- **"How I AI" series:** 2-3 episodes per week featuring leaders from high-profile companies (Figma, Coinbase, Vercel, Notion, Zapier, Webflow, Block, Gumroad). Published as articles with embedded video/podcast. Available on YouTube, Spotify, and Apple Podcasts. The podcast ranked #1 trending on Pocketcasts (June 2025).
- **Product updates ("What's New"):** Periodic changelog posts (roughly monthly). Not as frequent as the content series.
- **Resource guides:** 22+ evergreen guides covering "AI for PM," "Product Management," and "Templates & Frameworks."

### SEO Strategy Patterns
1. **Template-as-SEO play:** 24+ free templates targeting long-tail queries like "prd template," "go-to-market strategy template," "competitive analysis template." Each template has its own landing page.
2. **Resource hub as pillar content:** The /resources section with 22+ guides creates a content cluster around "AI product management" and "PRD writing." Individual guides target specific informational queries.
3. **Guest-driven content:** The "How I AI" series generates backlinks and social shares by featuring influential guests who amplify the content to their own audiences. This is a sophisticated earned-media strategy.
4. **Use-case landing pages:** Separate pages for Product Managers, Engineering, Design, Startups, and Enterprise use cases — targeting different buyer personas with tailored messaging.
5. **Integration landing pages:** Individual pages for each integration (Linear, MCP, etc.) that can capture "[tool] + chatprd" or "[tool] prd integration" queries.

### Content Gaps (observed)
- **No comparison pages.** No "ChatPRD vs. Claude" or "ChatPRD vs. Notion AI" pages found. This leaves competitor comparison queries to third-party review sites.
- **No case study landing pages.** Customer logos are shown (Autodesk, LaunchDarkly, etc.) but full case studies with metrics are not published as standalone pages.
- **Limited technical content.** No blog posts targeting engineering-specific queries (API docs, webhooks, data model) — because those capabilities do not exist.
- **No localization.** Content is English-only. No multi-language pages targeting non-English PM communities.
