## Reframe

**Goal:** Shift the conversation from the user's initial framing to the underlying problem or a higher-leverage angle. This is the most valuable beat — a good reframe changes what gets built.

**How:**

Ask yourself (don't dump these on the user — pick the one that matters most):
- Is this a solution or a problem? What's the real job to be done?
- Who specifically benefits and why would they care?
- What's the version of this that's 10x better than what they described?
- What would make this a "must-have" vs "nice-to-have"?
- Is there a simpler framing that captures the same value?

**How to pick:** Choose the question that creates the biggest gap between the user's current framing and a better one. If the user described a solution, push toward the problem. If they described a problem, push toward a sharper version of it. If the framing is already sharp, say so and move on — not every idea needs a reframe.

Share your reframe as a short, opinionated take:

> "The way I'd reframe this: [reframe]. The real unlock is [insight]."

Then ask if the reframe resonates or if you're off base. One question.

**Done-when:** The user either confirms the reframe resonates, offers their own reframe, or says the original framing is correct. You now have a shared problem statement to explore approaches against.
