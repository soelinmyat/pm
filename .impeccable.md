# Design Context — PM Plugin Artefacts

This file is read by the `impeccable` skill when generating any HTML artefact (proposals, RFCs, dashboards) for the PM plugin.

## Design Context

### Users
PM-engineers and hybrid roles reviewing product proposals before greenlighting engineering work. Workflow: open the artefact in a browser at a desk, read carefully (10–30 min), occasionally share the link with a non-CLI teammate. The reading mode is closer to "review a paper" than "scan a dashboard."

Secondary readers: non-engineer team members (designers, biz leads) who don't live in the terminal but need to follow product reasoning.

### Brand Personality
**Disciplined. Considered. Quiet.**

The PM plugin's core promise is "product context that compounds, not decays." Its artefacts should feel like work done with care — closer to a research memo or an editorial essay than a SaaS marketing page. Voice and tone are codified in `references/writing.md`: terse, verdict-first, no jargon, max 20 words per sentence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial document, not a dashboard. Light theme on warm-tinted neutrals. Strong typographic hierarchy carries the design — color, ornament, and chrome are restrained.

**References (the *feel*, not the components):**
- Stripe Press essays
- Tufte's print typography (sidenotes, numbered sections, generous margins)
- The Atlantic / NYT long-form web layouts
- Academic papers in physics or economics journals

**Anti-references:**
- Linear / Notion dashboard cards
- "AI-generated SaaS" — cyan-on-dark, gradient pills, glassmorphism
- The kind of admin UI where every section is a rounded card with a colored left-border stripe
- Maximalist hero blocks with big icons above every section heading

### Typography
Pairing chosen by deliberate procedure (not the reflex list):
- **Body:** Spectral (Production Type, via Google Fonts) — designed for long-form web reading
- **UI / labels / numbers:** Geist (Vercel, free) — quiet, technical, modern

Body type slightly larger than UI default (1.0625rem) since reading is the primary mode. Restrained type scale — H1 only ~2.25rem, no fluid clamping in this app context.

### Color
- Warm off-white background: `oklch(98% 0.005 60)`
- Ink: `oklch(20% 0.01 60)`
- Single accent (rare, used for the *one* thing that matters per page): deep ink-violet `oklch(38% 0.13 280)`
- Neutrals tinted toward the warm hue, not cool gray

### Design Principles
1. **Typography over chrome.** Hierarchy comes from font, weight, and whitespace — not from cards, borders, or icons.
2. **One annotation treatment, not ten card variants.** When something needs visual lift (a JTBD pull-quote, a callout), use the same subtle tinted block. Resist inventing new patterns.
3. **The accent is rare.** Ink-violet appears at most 2–3 times on a page. Overuse kills its power.
4. **Reading column stays narrow.** ~65ch for prose; tables and diagrams may extend to 880px.
5. **No AI-slop fingerprints.** No left-border stripes. No gradient text. No big icon-above-heading. No glassmorphism. No purple-to-blue gradients. No identical card grids.
